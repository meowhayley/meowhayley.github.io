Who We Are
Workshops
Several educational workshops are held in the garden. Workshop topics have ranged from kombucha-making to container gardening to medicinal herbs. Check our Facebook page for event updates and details!
Meetings
To keep SOGA running, out dedicated management team holds weekly leadership meetings to organize DeCals, development resistance efforts, garden projects, and potential events. Anyone is welcome to come to these meetings to help make decisions for SOGA. Our meeting schedule for Spring 2018 is TBD.
DeCals
DeCals are student-facilitated courses offered each semester for 1-3 units on a pass/no-pass basis. DeCals continue to be one of the main ways that the student community engages with the garden, and our spring course, Introduction to Organic Gardening and Food Justice (IOG), is one of the most popular DeCals on campus. In the fall, we offer Berkeley Urban Garden Internship (BUGI), Garden Leadership and Management (GLM), and People & Permaculture. All courses are held in the garden and facilitated by SOGA members.
Open Hours
Join us Sundays 10 am- 2 pm! This is the weekly window of time in which the garden gates will be open and at least one SOGA member will be there to facilitate garden tasks and projects. All community members are invited to come to the garden to help with garden tasks, learn some new gardening skills, harvest some free organic produce, mingle with other community gardeners, and relax under the fig tree. Garden tasks range from planting watermelon seeds in the greenhouse to pruning the apple trees to painting a mural on the side of the tool shed. All garden work is voluntary, and we highly suggest wearing closed-toed shoes, sunglasses, and sunscreen when volunteering!
